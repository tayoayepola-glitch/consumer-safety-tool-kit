# School Meal Safety

Children spend a significant portion of their day at school, making safe school meals critical for their health, growth, and learning. This guide provides practical tips to ensure meals provided at school or packed from home are safe and nutritious.

---

## Why School Meal Safety Matters
Unsafe meals can lead to:
- Foodborne illness outbreaks in classrooms
- Dehydration and malnutrition
- Increased absenteeism
- Long-term health issues

Safe meals support healthy development, concentration, and academic performance.

---

## Key Guidelines for Safe School Meals

### 1. Hand Hygiene
- Ensure children wash hands before eating  
- Encourage proper handwashing techniques among staff

### 2. Meal Preparation
- Prepare meals using clean utensils and surfaces  
- Cook foods thoroughly, especially proteins (meat, eggs, poultry)  
- Avoid raw or undercooked foods

### 3. Safe Storage
- Keep perishable meals refrigerated until consumption  
- Use insulated containers for packed meals  
- Discard leftovers that have been unrefrigerated for long periods

### 4. Avoid Cross-Contamination
- Separate raw and cooked foods  
- Use clean containers and lunchboxes  
- Wash fruits and vegetables before packing

### 5. Water and Drinks
- Provide safe, clean drinking water  
- Avoid giving children untreated or potentially contaminated beverages

### 6. Educate Children
- Encourage children to report food that smells, tastes, or looks unusual  
- Teach not to share meals with others if hygiene is uncertain

---

## Foods Requiring Extra Caution
- Raw or undercooked eggs, poultry, and meats  
- Unpasteurized milk or dairy products  
- Street-vended or unpackaged foods  
- Foods stored at room temperature for long periods  

---

## If a Child Shows Signs of Foodborne Illness
- Stop consumption immediately  
- Encourage hydration and rest  
- Notify school authorities and caregivers  
- Seek medical attention if necessary  
- Report suspected food safety issues to local authorities if possible  

---

## Public Health Note
Safe school meals reduce preventable illness, support child growth, and contribute to healthier communities globally. Schools, parents, and caregivers all play a role in ensuring safe meals for children.
