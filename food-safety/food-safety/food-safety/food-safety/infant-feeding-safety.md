# Infant Feeding Safety

Infants (0â€“12 months) have delicate immune systems and are highly susceptible to foodborne illnesses. Safe feeding practices are essential to protect their health, ensure proper growth, and prevent life-threatening infections.

---

## Why Infant Feeding Safety Matters
Unsafe feeding practices can result in:
- Gastrointestinal infections and diarrhea  
- Malnutrition or nutrient deficiencies  
- Hospitalization in severe cases  
- Long-term developmental impact  

Proper feeding practices support growth, immunity, and cognitive development.

---

## Key Guidelines for Infant Feeding Safety

### 1. Breastfeeding
- Exclusive breastfeeding is recommended for the first 6 months  
- Ensure proper hygiene when expressing or storing breast milk  
- Wash hands before feeding or handling milk storage equipment

### 2. Bottle Feeding
- Sterilize bottles, nipples, and feeding equipment before each use  
- Use safe, boiled water for formula preparation  
- Prepare formula fresh; avoid storing for long periods

### 3. Introducing Solid Foods
- Introduce solids gradually, starting at around 6 months  
- Wash fruits and vegetables thoroughly before pureeing or mashing  
- Avoid raw or undercooked animal products  

### 4. Food Storage
- Store prepared formula or solids in clean, airtight containers  
- Refrigerate leftovers immediately and discard after 24 hours  
- Never use food that looks, smells, or tastes spoiled

### 5. Avoid Cross-Contamination
- Keep raw and cooked foods separate  
- Use clean utensils, cutting boards, and feeding tools  
- Wash hands frequently before handling infant food

### 6. Water Safety
- Use safe, treated, or boiled water for infant food and formula  
- Avoid giving infants untreated or unsafe water  

---

## What To Do If an Infant Shows Signs of Illness
- Stop feeding suspected contaminated food or formula  
- Monitor for vomiting, diarrhea, fever, or lethargy  
- Seek medical attention immediately  
- Keep feeding logs and report unsafe products to caregivers or authorities  

---

## Public Health Note
Safe infant feeding practices prevent serious illness, promote healthy growth, and strengthen long-term public health outcomes globally. Parents, caregivers, and healthcare providers all play a vital role in infant food safety.
