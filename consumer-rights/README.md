# Consumer Rights: A Global Overview

This section outlines fundamental consumer rights that apply across many countries and regions, helping individuals understand what they are entitled to when purchasing goods and services.

## Why Consumer Rights Matter
Consumer rights protect individuals from:
- Unsafe or harmful products
- Unfair trade practices
- Misleading information and false claims
- Financial exploitation and fraud

Understanding these rights empowers consumers to make informed decisions and seek redress when issues arise.

## Core Consumer Rights (Globally Recognized)
While laws vary by country, the following rights are widely recognized:

### 1. Right to Safety
Consumers have the right to be protected from products that may pose risks to health or life.

### 2. Right to Information
Consumers are entitled to accurate, clear, and truthful information about products, including ingredients, usage instructions, and potential risks.

### 3. Right to Choice
Consumers should have access to a variety of products at competitive prices without coercion or unfair limitations.

### 4. Right to Be Heard
Consumers have the right to raise complaints and have their concerns addressed by sellers, manufacturers, or regulators.

### 5. Right to Redress
Consumers are entitled to refunds, replacements, or compensation for defective or unsafe products, in line with applicable laws.

### 6. Right to Consumer Education
Consumers have the right to knowledge and awareness needed to make informed purchasing decisions.

## Practical Steps When Your Rights Are Violated
- Stop using the product immediately if it is unsafe
- Document evidence (photos, receipts, batch numbers)
- Contact the seller or manufacturer
- Escalate to consumer protection agencies or regulators if unresolved

## Global Disclaimer
Consumer protection laws and enforcement mechanisms differ across countries. Always refer to your local consumer protection authority or regulatory agency for specific guidance.
